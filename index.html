About Me

In 2014, I started my path to a Computer Science degree at the University of California, Riverside. During my second year at UCR, I was a Peer Leader with the E.N.S.U.R.E program at the Bourns College of Engineering. In my third year, I took on a personal project to make a small incremental mobile game in the Unity Editor named Puppy Party. I taught myself how to use the Unity Editor and code in C#. This project was the catalyst for my drive to learn and apply different coding languages. In 2018, I successfully completed my engineering program and received my B.S. in Computer Science.

After graduating from UCR, I chose to pursue a path in secondary education focusing primarily on Mathematics and Computer Science. I started off as an Academic Manager with Franklin Educational Services and am currently a Mathematics and Computer Programming teacher with Fusion Academy Pasadena.
